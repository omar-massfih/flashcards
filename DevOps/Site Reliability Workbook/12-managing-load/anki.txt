Why do canaries improve load-balanced availability?	A small rollout limits regression impact, and health checks can route traffic away from failing new instances.
Why should load be measured close to the client?	Backend-visible traffic can hide refused connections, retries, or demand that never reached application servers.
What is a cascading failure in load management?	Overload in one layer causes retries, slowness, or resource exhaustion that increases load on other layers.
How can load balancer retries worsen overload?	Retrying timed-out requests adds more work to already slow or failing backends.
What is a thundering herd retry problem?	Many clients retry at nearly the same time, creating synchronized traffic spikes.
How do jitter and truncated exponential backoff reduce retry overload?	They spread retries over time and cap retry growth, preventing synchronized request spikes.
Why can slow backends be dangerous to load balancers?	They hold connections and trigger retries or reconnection pressure, consuming load-balancer resources.
Why can autoscaling improve availability and utilization?	It adds capacity when demand rises and can reduce waste when demand falls.
Why is autoscaling risky when misconfigured?	It can amplify bugs, overload dependencies, waste quota, or create feedback loops.
Why can unhealthy instances break utilization-based autoscaling?	If nonserving instances are included in the average, the autoscaler may think capacity is sufficient and fail to scale up.
How can load-balancer metrics improve autoscaling decisions?	Load balancers can report capacity using only healthy serving instances, excluding zombies or warming instances.
What is an autoscaler cool-down period?	A delay before new instances contribute metrics, giving them time to become healthy and representative.
Why must autohealing allow enough startup time?	Restarted instances may need time to load, warm up, or become healthy before another recovery action is useful.
Why does horizontal autoscaling often fail for overloaded stateful paths?	Session affinity can keep traffic pinned to hot backends, so adding instances may not move the overloaded sessions.
What helps stateful systems handle load better than simple horizontal scaling?	Task-level routing such as consistent hashing that spreads work more intelligently.
When can vertical autoscaling help stateful systems?	It can absorb short-term hotspots when combined with routing that balances work across tasks.
Why should vertical autoscaling be used cautiously?	It may grow low-traffic instances unnecessarily if scaling is applied uniformly.
Why should autoscaling usually be more aggressive upward than downward?	Failure to scale up can drop traffic, while slower scale-down protects spare capacity and stability.
Why should user-facing services reserve spare capacity?	Spare capacity gives autoscaling time to react and provides redundancy during failures or traffic spikes.
Why should autoscalers have minimum and maximum bounds?	Bounds prevent runaway scaling, quota exhaustion, and capacity plans that cannot be satisfied.
How can a CPU-based autoscaler waste quota?	A CPU-burning bug can cause repeated scale-ups even though no useful work is being done.
How can autoscaling overload a failing dependency?	Scaling callers upward can send more stuck requests to a dependency that is already failing.
Why do autoscalers need kill switches and manual overrides?	On-call engineers need fast, documented ways to stop bad scaling behavior and manually restore capacity.
Why analyze dependencies before enabling autoscaling?	Backends must be able to absorb extra traffic or degrade gracefully when frontends scale up.
Why can shared quota make autoscaling dangerous for microservices?	One service can consume quota during a spike, leaving dependent services unable to scale.
Why balance autoscaled instances across zones or regions?	It spreads quota use and failure domains instead of concentrating capacity in one location.
Why are load balancing, autoscaling, and load shedding not independent?	Each changes traffic or capacity signals that the others may use, creating feedback loops.
How can a load balancer account for shed requests?	Treat error or shed requests as overload signals rather than low-cost successful work.
Why should autoscaling usually happen before load shedding?	Otherwise the system may drop traffic it could have served after scaling up.
Why are RPC deadlines important for load management?	They stop servers and clients from wasting resources on requests that are no longer useful.
What happens when RPC requests lack useful deadlines?	Servers may hold resources until very large default limits, increasing latency and crash risk.
How should clients and servers handle stale RPC work?	Clients should cancel no-longer-useful requests, and servers should stop expensive work after deadlines expire.
Why should traffic-management systems be monitored together?	Their intersections can create runaway feedback loops invisible when each tool is observed alone.
