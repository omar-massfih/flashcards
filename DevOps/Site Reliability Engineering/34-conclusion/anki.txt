Why is splitting SRE time between operations and engineering so important?	It lets engineers both experience real system failures directly and build the tools or abstractions that make future operations easier.
Why does the conclusion compare SRE evolution to the aviation industry?	The analogy shows how much system complexity, safety, and scale can grow while the operator interface remains usable by a small, highly trained group.
Why must a compact SRE team still have deep system knowledge?	Abstractions and automation only work well when operators understand how the underlying systems behave and fail.
