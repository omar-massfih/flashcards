What is the main goal of embedding an SRE in an overloaded team?	To improve the team's habits, processes, and scalability mindset rather than merely helping clear the current queue.
What does "ops mode" mean in this chapter?	A way of running a service by adding more human effort as load grows instead of removing or automating the underlying scalability problems.
How does the SRE model differ from ops mode?	SRE aims to keep staffing from scaling directly with service load by using software, simplification, and better system design.
Why should an embedded SRE shadow on-call early in the engagement?	It reveals whether the team's scale assumptions are real and exposes the daily sources of stress and reactive work.
What is "kindling" in the context of operational overload?	Latent weaknesses or neglected risks that are likely to turn into future incidents or ongoing operational pain.
What are examples of operational kindling an embedded SRE should look for?	Knowledge gaps, noisy unactionable alerts, weak capacity plans, missing SLOs, shallow postmortems, and critical systems no one understands well.
Why should the embedded SRE write or co-write the next strong postmortem instead of critiquing old ones from afar?	Modeling a good blameless postmortem teaches the team what useful analysis looks like without putting them on the defensive.
Why is the Bad Apple Theory harmful in postmortem culture?	It wrongly assumes failures are caused mainly by flawed individuals instead of by systems that mislead or overload people.
Why should the embedded SRE sort team fires into toil and not-toil?	The distinction clarifies which work should be automated away and which work is legitimate operational overhead.
Why is defining an SLO often the first major step in fixing an overloaded team?	An SLO gives the team a quantitative way to judge impact, prioritize change, and move from reactive habits toward reliability-focused decisions.
What is a better pattern than personally fixing the team's problems during an embedding?	Find small high-value changes, explain why they matter, have team members implement them, and support through review and guidance.
Why is it important for the embedded SRE to explain reasoning explicitly?	The goal is to teach principles the team can reuse later, not just to impose isolated decisions they may cargo-cult.
What makes a good explanation for an SRE policy decision?	It ties the recommendation back to core principles such as SLOs, rollback safety, mean time to recovery, or correlated-failure risk.
Why are leading questions useful during an embedding?	They encourage the team to reason from SRE principles themselves instead of passively waiting for answers from the embedded engineer.
What should the final report from an embedded SRE engagement provide?	A summary of observed problems, the reasoning behind recommended changes, concrete examples, and action items for continued improvement.
