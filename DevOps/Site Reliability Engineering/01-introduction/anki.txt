What is a major indirect cost of a strict development/operations split?	Different incentives, vocabulary, and risk assumptions can create conflict, poor communication, and mistrust.
Why must an SRE team keep coding or it will drown?	Without engineering work, operational load grows with the service until the team scales linearly just to keep up.
What ops-work cap does Google place on SRE teams?	About 50% of total SRE time.
What happens when an SRE team spends too much time on operational work?	Google pushes some operational burden back to development or adds staffing rather than letting the team become pure ops.
Why does the SRE model avoid the usual dev/ops split pathology?	SRE shares software-engineering culture with development while still owning operational outcomes.
What is one major challenge of the SRE model?	Hiring is difficult because SRE requires both strong coding ability and useful systems knowledge.
Why is maintaining a durable focus on engineering a core SRE tenet?	Engineering effort is what reduces recurring operational work instead of merely absorbing it with more people.
How many significant events should an SRE typically handle per on-call shift?	At most about two per 8- to 12-hour shift.
Why is too much pager load dangerous for SRE teams?	Engineers become overwhelmed and lose time to investigate, learn from, and fix underlying problems.
Why should significant incidents have postmortems even if they never triggered a page?	They often reveal important monitoring gaps and systemic weaknesses.
What is a blameless postmortem?	An investigation focused on understanding causes and improving the system rather than blaming individuals.
What is an error budget?	The permitted amount of unreliability, equal to one minus the service's availability target.
Why do error budgets reduce conflict between product development and SRE?	They align both groups around maximizing feature velocity without exceeding an agreed reliability limit.
What is an alert in SRE?	A signal that a human must take immediate action.
What is a ticket in SRE?	A signal that a human should take action, but not immediately.
Why is email-style “someone should look at this” monitoring considered flawed in SRE?	Monitoring should already decide whether human action is required instead of asking a person to interpret raw signals.
What is the key metric for emergency response effectiveness in SRE?	Mean time to repair (MTTR).
Why do playbooks improve incident response?	They reduce improvisation and help engineers respond faster and more consistently under pressure.
Why does SRE treat change management as a primary reliability concern?	Most outages are caused by changes to a live system.
What practices make change management safer in SRE?	Progressive rollouts, fast detection of bad changes, and safe rollback mechanisms.
Why does automation improve change management?	It reduces human error and makes rollout and rollback faster and more consistent.
Why is capacity planning an SRE responsibility?	Capacity is tightly coupled to availability, so the team responsible for reliability must plan and provision it.
What should capacity planning account for?	Both organic growth and inorganic demand such as launches or marketing events.
Why must provisioning be handled carefully?	It is expensive, risky, and directly affects whether capacity will work when needed.
Why are efficiency and performance part of the SRE mission?	SRE controls provisioning and helps shape software behavior, so it has major leverage over cost and service capacity.
