Why is software engineering inside SRE so valuable?	SREs understand production constraints firsthand, so they can build internal tools that improve reliability, scalability, and operational efficiency more effectively than teams far from production.
Why does software development help SRE teams scale with service growth?	Automation and better tools reduce the amount of manual operational work, allowing supported services to grow faster than headcount.
Why can software projects be good for individual SREs as well as the organization?	They provide career growth, preserve coding skills, balance interrupt-driven work, and attract a wider mix of engineers into SRE.
What is the core weakness of traditional capacity planning?	It is brittle, laborious, and imprecise because humans must continually reshape concrete resource allocations as assumptions change.
Why is traditional capacity planning brittle to change?	Small changes in demand, efficiency, delivery dates, or requirements can invalidate allocations and force widespread rework across multiple planning periods.
Why is intent-based capacity planning more resilient than demand-only planning?	When supply, demand, or constraints change, the plan can be regenerated from the updated intent instead of manually patched in fragile ways.
Why is moving from implementation requests toward intent useful in capacity planning?	Higher-level intent exposes flexibility and trade-offs, making it easier to find better allocations and explain the consequences of unmet requirements.
Why are dependencies crucial to intent-based capacity planning?	Where one service can run is constrained by where its dependent services can run and by latency or locality requirements between them.
Why are performance metrics essential for capacity planning?	They translate higher-level demand into the lower-level resource usage required by dependent services and infrastructure.
Why must prioritization be explicit in intent-driven planning?	When capacity is insufficient, the system needs transparent rules for which requirements or service goals should be sacrificed first.
Why was Auxon a good SRE-led software project?	The team both understood the production pain deeply and directly felt the consequences when the tool failed or succeeded.
Why is approximation often a good early strategy in SRE software projects?	A simpler initial solution can prove the concept and create momentum while the team learns the harder parts of the problem space.
How should teams use approximation without trapping themselves later?	Abstract the approximated component behind a clean interface so it can be replaced as understanding and requirements mature.
Why is modularity especially important when requirements are fuzzy?	It lets teams keep shipping while preserving the ability to swap implementations and adapt as the surrounding ecosystem changes.
Why was agnosticism an important design principle for Auxon?	Accepting many data sources and surrounding tools lowered adoption friction and kept the system broadly useful across divergent teams.
Why is launch and iterate especially important for SRE software projects?	Production problems and requirements are often partly unknown, so shipping something useful early creates learning and credibility faster than waiting for a perfect design.
Why do internal SRE tools still need strong product thinking?	Even expert internal users need usable workflows, documentation, support, and a believable roadmap or they will build their own alternatives.
Why is choosing the right early customers important for an internal SRE product?	Teams with real pain and no entrenched solution are more likely to adopt early, prove value, and become advocates.
Why can white-glove support be worthwhile for early adopters of an internal tool?	It reduces onboarding friction, addresses emotional resistance to automation, and helps create successful reference users.
Why should engineers building SRE software remain active in production work?	Staying embedded in operations keeps the team grounded in real problems and preserves the perspective that makes SRE-built tools valuable.
Why is introducing software development into SRE an organizational change, not just a technical one?	It requires new habits, roles, expectations, and coordination beyond the usual reflex of solving local pain with ad hoc scripts.
What capabilities may an SRE organization need to add for successful product-style development?	Product management, agile process leadership, customer advocacy, and other skills common on software product teams.
Why should SRE software efforts deliver visible value early?	Early useful releases build credibility, attract support, and make it easier to sustain staffing and broader adoption.
Why should SRE software teams hold themselves to normal production standards?	If an internal tool becomes critical to how SREs work, weak review, testing, or readiness practices will quickly destroy trust in the effort.
