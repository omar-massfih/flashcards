What is the first step in custom architecture trade-off analysis?	Find the dimensions or system parts that are entangled with one another.
Why identify entangled dimensions before comparing architecture options?	It defines the real decision space and prevents analyzing choices in isolation.
What practical question reveals coupling between two architecture parts?	If X changes, could Y be forced to change?
What does a static coupling diagram show?	The structural dependencies an architecture quantum needs in order to run.
What belongs in a static coupling diagram for a service?	Platform dependencies, library/framework dependencies, persistence dependencies, bootstrap integration points, and required messaging infrastructure.
How can build automation help with coupling analysis?	It can emit dependency and integration metadata as systems are generated or deployed.
Why build sample topologies during trade-off analysis?	They let architects test "what if" scenarios and see coupling effects before committing to an implementation.
What is the goal of modeling coupling-point combinations?	To discover which forces require trade-off analysis and which combinations are infeasible.
Why rate each design option independently before consolidating results?	It reduces comparison bias and makes the later matrix easier to interpret.
What can a consolidated trade-off matrix reveal?	Patterns and correlations, such as tighter coupling usually reducing scale and responsiveness.
Why are architecture trade-off tables often qualitative rather than quantitative?	Real architectures differ too much for precise numeric comparisons to transfer cleanly.
How can architects strengthen qualitative trade-off analysis?	Use representative examples, local spikes, existing system evidence, and objective fitness functions.
What is the out-of-context trap in architecture decisions?	Choosing from generic pros and cons without applying the specific constraints of the problem.
How can context reverse an architecture decision?	A locally critical constraint can outweigh the option that looks best in a generic comparison.
Why does narrowing the decision context simplify architecture design?	It removes irrelevant options and focuses attention on the trade-offs that can actually change the outcome.
Why model relevant domain scenarios during trade-off analysis?	Domain scenarios expose the forces that matter to the real system rather than an abstract pattern.
What can scenario modeling reveal that generic analysis often hides?	Workflow coordination, data consistency, security, operations, and failure-mode costs.
What do simple change scenarios often reveal in service-splitting decisions?	Separate services can improve maintainability, testability, deployability, and extensibility.
What do complex workflow scenarios often reveal in service-splitting decisions?	Splitting services may introduce coordination, performance, and data consistency costs.
How should architects present trade-offs to nontechnical stakeholders?	Reduce the analysis to the few outcome-level choices stakeholders can meaningfully judge.
What is a useful bottom-line framing for synchronous versus asynchronous workflows?	Whether the business values immediate start guarantees more than responsiveness and fault tolerance.
Why is technology evangelism risky in architecture?	It tends to amplify benefits, minimize costs, and hide trade-offs until they reappear later.
How should architects respond to silver-bullet technology claims?	Ask for the specific advantages, disadvantages, constraints, and failure modes in the local context.
How can scenario analysis counter architecture evangelism?	It tests claimed benefits against real workflows instead of debating generic preferences.
Why can past architecture success become misleading?	A solution that worked in one narrow context may fail under different business drivers or constraints.
How can fitness functions support experimental architecture decisions?	They objectively detect whether expected risks, such as accidental coupling, are appearing.
What role do metrics play in trade-off analysis?	They turn speculation into measurable evidence that can refine or challenge qualitative judgments.
Why keep writing trade-off tables and ADRs after a successful architecture migration?	They preserve decision discipline, communicate rationale, and make future changes easier to evaluate.
