Why do operational and analytical data need different architectural treatment?	Operational data supports transactions and current system behavior, while analytical data supports reporting, prediction, ML, and strategic decisions.
What problem did the data warehouse pattern try to solve?	Consolidate operational data from many systems into a separate store optimized for analytical queries.
Why do data warehouses often transform operational schemas?	Operational schemas fit transactions, while analytical schemas often need denormalized structures for reporting and aggregation.
What is a star schema?	A dimensional model that separates measurable facts from descriptive dimensions.
Why are star schemas denormalized?	To simplify queries, reduce joins, speed aggregations, and support multidimensional analysis.
What is fact data in a star schema?	Quantifiable business measures such as rates, durations, counts, distances, or repair times.
What is dimension data in a star schema?	Descriptive attributes that give context to facts, such as names, locations, categories, or specialties.
Why is a data warehouse technically partitioned?	It organizes data around analytical technical concerns rather than preserving operational domain boundaries.
Why are data warehouses brittle integrations?	Operational schema changes must be reflected in extraction, transformation, and warehouse schemas.
Why do data warehouses partition domain knowledge poorly?	Operational teams and analytics teams both need domain understanding, but the transformed warehouse separates data from its original context.
Why can data warehouses become complex ecosystems?	They require separate storage, compute, schemas, ingestion, transformation, tooling, and maintenance outside operational systems.
Why did many data warehouses provide limited business value?	They were expensive to build and maintain, yet often could not answer new analytical questions without more specialized work.
How can data warehouse synchronization create bottlenecks?	Many operational data streams must converge through extraction and transformation pipelines that can slow teams and affect source systems.
What problem did the data lake pattern try to address?	Avoid expensive up-front transformations by storing data closer to its native form and transforming it only when needed.
Why can data lakes fit machine learning better than data warehouses?	ML often benefits from data closer to raw or semi-raw form rather than a heavily transformed analytical schema.
Why is data discovery difficult in a data lake?	Domain relationships and meaning can be lost when raw data is dumped into a centralized, loosely structured store.
Why can data lakes increase PII and privacy risk?	Raw or loosely organized data can be combined in ways that reveal sensitive or identifying information.
Why are data lakes still technically partitioned?	They centralize ingestion, storage, transformation, and serving capabilities rather than aligning ownership with domains.
Why can data lakes still suffer from stale data?	Batch-like ingestion and coupled pipelines can lag or break when upstream systems change.
What quality burden do data lakes often push downstream?	Data integrity, quality checks, cleansing, and transformation move to lake consumers or downstream pipelines.
What is data mesh?	A sociotechnical approach for decentralized sharing, access, and management of analytical data aligned to business domains.
What are the four principles of data mesh?	Domain ownership of data; data as a product; self-serve data platform; computational federated governance.
What does domain ownership mean in data mesh?	The domain that creates or deeply understands data owns and shares its analytical data product.
What does "data as a product" mean in data mesh?	Domains serve data with consumer-oriented quality, discoverability, security, timeliness, and usability.
Why does data mesh need a self-serve data platform?	Domain teams need platform capabilities to create, discover, govern, and consume data products without a centralized data team bottleneck.
What is computational federated governance?	Organization-wide data policies are decided federatively and automated as code inside data products.
What is a data product quantum?	An operationally independent architecture quantum that packages analytical data and behavior for a domain.
How does a data product quantum relate to a service?	It sits adjacent to the domain service, highly coupled to its domain data but operationally separate.
What is a source-aligned data product quantum?	A DPQ that exposes analytical data on behalf of the domain service or architecture quantum that originates it.
What is an aggregate data product quantum?	A DPQ that combines data from multiple source data products.
What is a fit-for-purpose data product quantum?	A DPQ built for a specific analytical, BI, ML, reporting, or supporting use case.
What is a cooperative quantum in data mesh?	An operationally separate quantum that cooperates with a service through tight local contracts and eventual consistency.
Why should DPQs communicate with operational services asynchronously?	Analytics should minimally affect operational performance, availability, and transaction behavior.
Why should DPQs use eventual consistency with operational data?	Requiring analytical and operational data to stay transactionally synchronized defeats the decoupling purpose of data mesh.
How does data mesh preserve domain context better than warehouses or lakes?	Data products remain owned and shaped by the domains that understand the data.
How does data mesh reduce centralized analytics bottlenecks?	Data ownership and product creation are distributed to domains, while the platform supplies shared capabilities and governance.
What is a key trade-off of data mesh?	It improves domain-aligned analytical decoupling but requires contract coordination, platform maturity, and federated governance.
Why can loose contracts be useful between DPQs?	They reduce brittleness as source domains evolve while still allowing consumer-driven validation where needed.
What should a data product expose besides data?	Metadata, quality expectations, access policies, lineage, freshness, and usage-oriented interfaces.
How can data mesh address PII risk better than a data lake?	Domain owners and policy sidecars can enforce privacy and access rules at the point of data product access.
What role does a governance sidecar play in a DPQ?	It stores and executes governance policies such as access control, compliance, privacy, and quality checks.
