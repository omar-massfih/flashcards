What is a transactional saga?	A sequence of local transactions across services, with later steps or compensating actions used to complete or repair the overall workflow.
Why are saga errors architecturally interesting?	Distributed transactions lack true business-request atomicity, so failure handling determines whether the workflow can recover safely.
What three dimensions define transactional saga patterns?	Communication, consistency, and coordination.
What are the two communication choices in saga patterns?	Synchronous and asynchronous communication.
What are the two consistency choices in saga patterns?	Atomic consistency and eventual consistency.
What are the two coordination choices in saga patterns?	Orchestrated coordination and choreographed coordination.
What does a saga pattern superscript encode?	The saga's communication, consistency, and coordination choices.
What is the Epic Saga pattern?	A synchronous, atomic, orchestrated saga.
Why does the Epic Saga feel familiar to many architects?	It mimics monolithic transactional behavior with a central workflow owner and all-or-nothing coordination.
What is the main advantage of the Epic Saga?	It provides clear transactional coordination and a clear workflow owner.
Why is the Epic Saga highly coupled?	It combines synchronous communication, atomic consistency, and orchestrated coordination.
What is a major operational drawback of the Epic Saga?	The orchestrator must coordinate all participants before responding, hurting responsiveness, availability, scale, and elasticity.
What is a compensating update?	An operation that attempts to undo a committed write, such as reversing an update or deleting an inserted row.
Why are compensating updates difficult in distributed systems?	They must repair already committed local transactions without true isolation across services.
What is a side effect in compensating transactions?	Another service may already have acted on data before the compensation reverses the original change.
Why can compensation failure confuse users?	The original partial change may remain committed while the user is told the overall operation failed or should be retried.
Why can atomic distributed transactions unnecessarily couple users to backend workflow details?	The user may receive failure because a secondary workflow step failed, even if their main task succeeded.
How do compensating updates affect responsiveness?	The caller may wait while the system detects failure and attempts corrective actions.
What is the Phone Tag Saga pattern?	A synchronous, atomic, choreographed saga.
Why does Phone Tag Saga need workflow logic inside domain services?	Atomicity must be coordinated somewhere, and there is no orchestrator to own it.
When is Phone Tag Saga most plausible?	For simple workflows needing somewhat better scale than orchestration and having easy error handling.
What is the main drawback of Phone Tag Saga?	Error handling and compensation logic spread across participating services.
What is the Fairy Tale Saga pattern?	A synchronous, eventual-consistency, orchestrated saga.
Why is Fairy Tale Saga often attractive?	It keeps a mediator for workflow control while avoiding holistic distributed transactionality.
How does Fairy Tale Saga handle service-local transactions?	Each domain service owns its own transaction, while the orchestrator manages workflow progress and repair.
What is the main trade-off of Fairy Tale Saga?	It improves simplicity and responsiveness but accepts that data may be temporarily inconsistent.
What is the Time Travel Saga pattern?	A synchronous, eventual-consistency, choreographed saga.
What kind of workflow fits Time Travel Saga well?	Simple one-way workflows such as chain-of-responsibility, pipes-and-filters, ingestion, or bulk-processing flows.
Why does Time Travel Saga become hard for complex workflows?	Without an orchestrator, each service must manage workflow state, error paths, and eventual consistency.
What is the Fantasy Fiction Saga pattern?	An asynchronous, atomic, orchestrated saga.
Why is Fantasy Fiction Saga usually unattractive?	It tries to combine asynchronous communication with atomic coordination, creating complex pending-state and ordering problems.
What problems does asynchronous atomic orchestration introduce?	The orchestrator must handle race conditions, out-of-order events, pending transactions, and dependency conflicts.
What is the Horror Story Saga pattern?	An asynchronous, atomic, choreographed saga.
Why is Horror Story Saga considered an anti-pattern?	It requires atomic consistency without either synchronous ordering or a mediator, forcing services to coordinate complex undo logic asynchronously.
Why might architects accidentally choose Horror Story Saga?	They try to improve Epic Saga performance with asynchronicity and choreography while keeping atomic transactionality.
What is the better direction than Horror Story Saga for high throughput?	Usually move toward eventual consistency, such as Anthology Saga, instead of preserving holistic atomicity.
What is the Parallel Saga pattern?	An asynchronous, eventual-consistency, orchestrated saga.
Why is Parallel Saga attractive for complex high-scale workflows?	It keeps an orchestrator for workflow control while using asynchronous communication and service-local transactions.
How does Parallel Saga handle errors?	The mediator can issue asynchronous remediation, retries, synchronization, or compensating messages.
What is the main trade-off of Parallel Saga?	It scales and responds well but must manage asynchronous timing, queues, race conditions, and eventual consistency.
What is the Anthology Saga pattern?	An asynchronous, eventual-consistency, choreographed saga.
Why is Anthology Saga the least coupled saga pattern?	It uses asynchronous communication, eventual consistency, and no central orchestrator.
What kind of workflow fits Anthology Saga best?	Simple, mostly linear, high-throughput workflows with simple or infrequent error cases.
Why is Anthology Saga difficult for complex workflows?	Coordination, error handling, and workflow context must live in the participating services or message contracts.
What makes atomic consistency the hardest saga dimension?	It requires distributed participants to behave as one all-or-nothing unit despite independent local commits and failures.
Why does eventual consistency often improve saga responsiveness?	The user can receive a successful response while background repair, retries, or manual handling resolves secondary failures.
What is state management in an eventual-consistency saga?	Tracking the saga's current state and using retries, analysis, or manual repair to move it toward completion.
How does state management differ from compensating updates?	State management records and repairs incomplete progress; compensating updates try to undo prior committed work.
What is a saga state machine?	A model of all possible saga states, transitions, and actions in a distributed workflow.
Why are saga state machines useful?	They make valid transitions, retry states, error states, and completion paths explicit.
What should happen when an eventual-consistency saga enters an error state?	The system should retry, analyze, or escalate for manual repair until the saga reaches a valid completion state.
Why should saga state be known even without atomic transactions?	Teams need to understand whether the workflow is complete, pending, failed, being retried, or awaiting repair.
What is the trade-off of state management over atomic compensation?	It improves responsiveness and user experience but allows temporary inconsistency.
Why can't distributed transactions be dropped in like a framework?	They require application-specific workflow, state, error-handling, and compensation logic designed into the services.
How can code annotations or attributes help manage sagas?	They document which services participate in which sagas and make the information discoverable by tools.
Why is saga participation metadata useful?	It helps identify testing scope, affected services, and workflow impact when a service or saga changes.
What can a saga code-walking tool report?	The services involved in a named saga by scanning service entry points and saga annotations.
How should architects choose a transactional saga pattern?	Compare communication timing, consistency needs, coordination complexity, error frequency, responsiveness, scale, and semantic coupling.
