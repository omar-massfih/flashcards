Why are negotiation skills critical for software architects?	Architectural decisions are constantly challenged, so architects must resolve disagreements and gain support from stakeholders, peers, and developers.
Why should architects pay attention to buzzwords and jargon during negotiation?	Exaggerated phrases often reveal the stakeholder's real concerns, such as time to market, performance, or availability.
Why should architects gather as much information as possible before a negotiation?	Better facts make it easier to frame the trade-offs clearly and avoid arguing from incomplete assumptions.
Why can expressing availability in downtime terms be better than using "nines" language?	Hours, minutes, and seconds make the trade-off more concrete and easier for stakeholders to reason about.
Why should cost and time usually be a last-resort negotiation tactic?	Leading with money or schedule can put people on the defensive before you understand what concern matters most to them.
What does "divide and conquer" mean in architectural negotiation?	Narrow a broad demand to the specific part of the system that truly needs it, reducing scope and cost.
Why is demonstration often better than argument in technical negotiations?	Showing results in a production-like environment defeats abstract debate and reveals what actually works in context.
Why should architects avoid letting technical disagreements become personal?	Calm, clear reasoning preserves collaboration, while personal conflict damages trust and decision quality.
Why is providing justification important when negotiating with developers?	Developers are more likely to accept a constraint or decision when they understand the reason behind it.
Why is it often better to state the reason before the demand in a negotiation?	People stop listening once they hear something they resist, so leading with justification improves the chance that they hear it.
Why can collaborative phrasing improve negotiations with developers?	Questions and explanations keep the conversation open, while commands shut collaboration down.
How can an architect get developer buy-in from disagreement?	Let the developer test or prove the alternative themselves so they either validate it or discover why it fails.
Why should architects strive to be both pragmatic and visionary?	They must think strategically about the future while still delivering practical solutions that fit real constraints.
What does being pragmatic mean for a software architect?	Accounting realistically for budget, time, team skills, trade-offs, and technical limitations.
How does leading by example differ from leading by title?	Respect is earned through behavior, collaboration, and support rather than through authority alone.
Why is language important in architectural leadership?	Small wording choices can either invite collaboration or make people defensive and resistant.
What is one sign that an architect is integrating well with a development team?	The team sees the architect as a go-to person for questions, help, and guidance.
Why should architects ask whether they are truly needed in a meeting?	Unnecessary meetings pull them away from mentoring, guiding, and unblocking the development team.
Why are meeting agendas useful for architects?	They help decide whether to attend, whether only part of the meeting matters, or whether notes are enough.
