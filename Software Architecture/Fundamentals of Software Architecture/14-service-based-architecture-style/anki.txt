What is the service-based architecture style?	A distributed architecture style built from separately deployed, coarse-grained domain services, usually paired with a UI layer and often a shared database.
Why is service-based architecture considered a pragmatic distributed style?	It delivers modularity and independent deployment with much less complexity and cost than finer-grained distributed styles such as microservices.
What are domain services in service-based architecture?	Coarse-grained services aligned to a business domain or subdomain, such as ordering, shipping, or invoicing.
Why are domain services usually coarse-grained in service-based architecture?	Coarser service boundaries keep related business logic and data changes together, reducing coordination overhead between services.
How is request orchestration usually handled in service-based architecture?	A domain service's API facade usually orchestrates the internal business steps needed to fulfill the request.
How does request orchestration in service-based architecture differ from microservices?	Service-based architecture usually orchestrates work inside a coarse-grained service, while microservices often coordinate multiple remote fine-grained services.
Why can service-based architecture usually rely on ACID transactions?	Because related work often stays inside one coarse-grained domain service, allowing normal database commits and rollbacks within that service boundary.
What transaction trade-off separates service-based architecture from microservices?	Service-based architecture favors local ACID transactions inside a domain service, while microservices often need eventual consistency and compensating actions across services.
What role can an API Gateway play in service-based architecture?	It can centralize cross-cutting concerns such as security, metrics, auditing, service discovery, and load balancing.
What is a major risk of using a shared monolithic database in service-based architecture?	Schema changes can ripple across many services, making changes costly, risky, and coordination-heavy.
Why is a single shared entity library an antipattern in service-based architecture?	A schema change can force rebuilding and redeploying every service, even those that do not actually use the changed tables.
How can logical database partitioning reduce change impact in service-based architecture?	Partitioning the schema by domain and matching it with separate shared libraries limits retesting and redeployment to affected services.
Why should interservice communication usually be minimized in service-based architecture?	The style works best when domains are largely independent and most coordination happens inside services or at the UI or gateway layer.
What does excessive interservice communication usually indicate in service-based architecture?	That the domain boundaries are poorly partitioned or that a different architecture style may be a better fit.
Why should service-based architecture usually avoid having too many domain services?	Too many services increase testing, deployment, monitoring, and database-change complexity; a practical upper limit is about 12.
Why do technically partitioned teams fit poorly with service-based architecture?	Requirements usually cut across UI, business, and data work inside one domain, forcing heavy interteam coordination in technically siloed organizations.
Why do scalability and elasticity score lower in service-based architecture than in microservices?	Scaling a coarse-grained service replicates more functionality than scaling a smaller single-purpose service, making it less efficient and less cost-effective.
Why is service-based architecture a natural fit for domain-driven design?	Each coarse-grained service can map cleanly to a domain boundary, making domain logic easier to isolate, change, and deploy.
Why can service-based architecture be a good stepping stone toward microservices?	It lets teams first find stable domain boundaries, then split only the domains that truly need finer-grained services.
When is service-based architecture a good choice?	When a system needs domain modularity, independent deployment, and solid fault isolation without the full complexity of highly distributed fine-grained architectures.
