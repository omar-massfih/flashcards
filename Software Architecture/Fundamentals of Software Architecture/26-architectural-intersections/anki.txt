Why can a technically sound architecture still fail without proper intersections?	If implementation, infrastructure, data, teams, or business context are misaligned, the architecture's intended qualities will not materialize.
How can implementation drift undermine architectural characteristics?	Developers may optimize for local concerns like responsiveness or convenience in ways that break the architecture's intended scalability, elasticity, or decoupling.
Why must source-code structure align with logical architecture?	When code organization diverges from the logical design, maintainability, testability, deployability, and evolvability all suffer.
Why are automated governance tools useful for implementation alignment?	They help enforce structural rules and constraints so the implementation does not quietly drift away from the intended architecture.
Why can violating architectural constraints cause the architecture to fail?	The style may rely on those restrictions to preserve change control, decoupling, performance, or some other critical quality.
Why is the intersection of architecture and infrastructure so important?	Operational characteristics such as scalability, elasticity, availability, and fault tolerance depend on infrastructure as much as on design.
How can cloud deployment still misalign with architecture even when cloud features are powerful?	Poor placement or configuration choices can negate benefits like performance, fault tolerance, elasticity, or data consistency.
Why must database topology align with architectural style?	The wrong topology can weaken the style's intended superpowers, such as bounded context, change control, scalability, or fault tolerance.
Why is database-per-service usually aligned with microservices?	It preserves bounded contexts and supports independent change, deployment, and ownership.
Why should architects align database type with architectural characteristics?	Different databases amplify different qualities, so choosing one that supports the architecture's key characteristics strengthens the whole system.
Why should architects align database choice with data structure and read/write priorities?	Misalignment between data shape or access pattern and database type leads to inefficiency and poor performance.
Why can engineering practices determine whether an architecture succeeds?	Some styles assume mature practices such as automation, testing, continuous delivery, and operational collaboration.
Why is microservices a poor fit for weak engineering practices?	Its benefits depend on automation, deployment maturity, testing discipline, and strong operational support.
How can fitness functions help align engineering practices with architecture?	They measure whether the system is actually preserving the architectural characteristics that the practices are supposed to support.
Why must team topology align with architecture?	If team structure fights the architecture's partitioning model, teams will struggle to build, change, and maintain the system effectively.
How does partitioning type connect architecture and team topology?	Domain-partitioned architectures work best with domain-aligned teams, while technically partitioned architectures align better with technically specialized teams.
Why is systems integration an important architectural intersection?	External systems can impose limits on compatibility, reliability, scalability, responsiveness, and coupling.
Why must architecture align with enterprise standards and practices?	A solution that ignores enterprise constraints may be rejected or become an unsustainable one-off even if it is technically strong.
What is domain-to-architecture isomorphism in the business intersection?	The fit between the business environment and the generic shape or style of the architecture.
Why is business change especially hard for architecture?	Architects must handle both known unknowns and unknown unknowns, so rigid upfront design inevitably breaks down.
Why are iterative and evolutionary approaches important for business alignment?	They let the architecture adapt as business needs change instead of assuming everything important is known in advance.
