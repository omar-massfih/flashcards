Why should architects prioritize technical breadth over technical depth?	They must evaluate many technologies, trade-offs, and contexts across a system rather than specialize deeply in only one area.
What is the difference between coupling and cohesion?	Coupling measures interdependence between parts, while cohesion measures how strongly the responsibilities within one part belong together.
Why is an architectural quantum important?	It helps identify which parts of a system can vary independently in operational characteristics and which parts are still coupled together.
What is the difference between technical partitioning and domain partitioning?	Technical partitioning groups by technical role such as UI or data access, while domain partitioning groups by business capability or workflow.
What distinguishes an architectural style from an architectural pattern?	A style defines a broader named topology and structure, while a pattern is a contextualized solution to a recurring problem.
When does a distributed architecture tend to be preferable to a monolith?	When different parts of the system need materially different characteristics, boundaries, or scaling behavior.
Why is bounded context so important in microservices?	It preserves service independence by keeping code, data, and business meaning private to each service boundary.
Why is data isolation critical in microservices?	Shared data structures create coupling that undermines bounded contexts, independent change, and deployability.
What is the key difference between choreography and orchestration?	Choreography distributes workflow coordination among participants, while orchestration centralizes it in a mediator.
What are the core sections of an Architectural Decision Record?	Title, Status, Context, Decision, and Consequences.
What is the purpose of a risk matrix in architecture?	To combine impact and likelihood into a more objective way of assessing risk.
What is domain-to-architecture isomorphism?	The fit between the shape of the business problem and the shape of the architecture.
Why must trade-off analysis be repeated instead of done only once?	Context changes, and small differences in goals or environment can change which option is best.
