Why is collaboration between architects and developers critical?	Architecture succeeds only when decisions flow both ways and the people implementing the system can clarify, challenge, and refine them.
Why does a one-way handoff from architect to developers usually fail?	Important decisions get lost, implementation feedback never returns, and the architecture drifts away from reality.
What is one key role of an architect in relation to the development team?	Create and communicate the right constraints so developers have guidance without being boxed in or abandoned.
What happens when architectural constraints are too tight?	Developers lose flexibility, become frustrated, and cannot use the tools or approaches they need to implement the system effectively.
What happens when architectural constraints are too loose?	Developers are forced to make too many architectural decisions themselves, causing confusion, proofs of concept, and reduced productivity.
What is a control-freak architect?	An architect who over-specifies implementation details and imposes excessive constraints on the development team.
Why is a control-freak architect usually ineffective?	They steal design ownership from developers, create frustration, and prevent the team from solving implementation problems in its own way.
What is an armchair architect?	An architect who is too detached from the technology, business domain, or team to provide useful guidance.
Why is an armchair architect usually ineffective?	They create boundaries that are too loose, forcing developers to fill architectural gaps without enough direction.
What is elastic leadership in software architecture?	Adjusting the architect's level of involvement based on the team and project context rather than always leading the same way.
What five factors help determine how involved an architect should be with a team?	Team familiarity, team size, overall experience, project complexity, and project duration.
What is process loss in team effectiveness?	The gap between a team's potential productivity and its actual productivity caused by coordination overhead.
Why can adding more people to a project reduce team effectiveness?	More people create more coordination and communication overhead, which can outweigh the extra capacity.
What is pluralistic ignorance in a team setting?	When people privately doubt a decision but stay silent because they assume everyone else sees something they missed.
Why should an architect watch for pluralistic ignorance in meetings?	It can hide weak decisions because no one feels safe enough to challenge the group.
What is diffusion of responsibility in a software team?	As teams get larger, people assume someone else will handle an issue, so responsibilities become less clear and work gets dropped.
What kinds of warning signs suggest a team may be too large?	Process loss, pluralistic ignorance, and diffusion of responsibility.
When are checklists especially useful for software teams?	When tasks are frequently missed or error-prone and do not depend on a rigid procedural order.
When are checklists a poor fit?	When the work is procedural with dependent steps or already routine and reliably performed without error.
Why should architects avoid overusing checklists?	Too many checklists trigger diminishing returns, and developers start ignoring them.
Why should checklist items be automated when possible?	Automation shortens checklists, removes toil, and improves the signal-to-noise ratio.
Why should architects communicate design principles rather than just isolated decisions?	Principles give developers a framework for making good local choices within architectural boundaries.
How can requiring technical and business justification improve a team's decisions?	It pushes developers to think not only about elegance or preference, but also about value, cost, and impact on the organization.
