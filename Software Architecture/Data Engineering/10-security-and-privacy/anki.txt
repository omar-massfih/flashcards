Why should security be treated as a first concern in data engineering?	Data engineers handle sensitive data, credentials, and access paths, so one breach can destroy trust and seriously damage the business.
Why is privacy a major concern for data engineers beyond ethics?	Privacy laws and regulations can impose severe legal and financial consequences for mishandling sensitive data.
Why are people often considered the weakest link in security?	Human error, phishing, carelessness, and poor judgment often compromise systems more easily than technical controls do.
What is the power of negative thinking in security?	Deliberately imagining failure and attack scenarios so you can prevent them instead of assuming everything will be fine.
Why is not collecting sensitive data often the best privacy protection?	Data that is never ingested cannot be leaked, misused, or exposed later.
Why should engineers be deeply skeptical of requests for credentials or sensitive information?	Attackers often succeed by impersonation or social engineering rather than by breaking systems directly.
What is security theater?	Performing security mainly for compliance optics rather than creating real protection against likely threats.
What is active security?	A proactive approach that studies real threats and organizational vulnerabilities instead of relying only on static checklists.
What is a broken-glass process for sensitive data?	An emergency-only access path with explicit approval and immediate revocation after the urgent work is done.
Why do many cloud security breaches still happen on the customer side?	Misconfigurations, excessive permissions, and operational mistakes are usually the customer's responsibility.
Why is encryption necessary but not sufficient for security?	Encryption helps against interception and storage theft, but compromised credentials or bad permissions can still expose data.
Why is key handling as important as encryption itself?	Poorly protected keys can nullify the protection that encryption is supposed to provide.
Why can unexpected billing spikes indicate a security problem?	Compromised accounts or malicious workloads may consume compute or storage in unusual ways.
